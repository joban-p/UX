Chapter 1

What is expense management?

Expense management is the system and process you use to pay and report on expense claims — from train tickets to stationery to SaaS subscription services. Thanks to technology, there are ways to make this process significantly easier, more cost-effective and way more time-efficient.

Many small businesses are surprised at the sheer effort involved in tracking and auditing this constant stream of small but significant payments, which is why many don't do it well. It gets particularly complex when you also have staff to worry about.

Four reasons expense management matters

Expenses are a sizeable cost

Companies spend on average 6–12% of their total annual budget on travel and entertainment alone. The cost of processing those claims varies wildly: from under $7 for automated systems to over $26 for manual processes.

Companies that lose track lose money

A Soldo survey of 2,500 employees revealed that over a third claimed for things they shouldn't, averaging £117 in fraudulent claims each month. Controlling company spending is one of the biggest challenges SMEs face.

Compliance is non-negotiable

To claim back tax on employee business expenses, you must prove to HMRC they are genuine. Making Tax Digital (MTD) makes this more important than ever. Businesses can't claim VAT without proper receipts — on average, 8% of VAT is lost.

Hybrid working creates new challenges

The shift to hybrid working increases fraud risk: employees may share corporate cards, and phishing emails impersonating CFOs or CEOs have risen dramatically. Robust expense management is a defence as well as an efficiency tool.

The three traditional methods — and why they fail

Many small businesses still operate a petty cash fund, which employees dip into for small value purchases, replacing cash with receipts. Despite its continuing popularity, the system has serious drawbacks.

  • Security risk — cash can be stolen or misappropriated with no clear audit trail
  • Time-consuming — all transactions must be written up and recorded by hand
  • Cloud software requires reposting ledgers manually, unlike automated bank feeds
  • Money is given out before spend, increasing the risk of loss or misuse

The most common method for SMEs. Employees lay out personal funds and reclaim them later via monthly spreadsheet submissions. This is also the most inefficient approach of the three.

  • Damages employee cash flow — staff wait weeks for reimbursement
  • Spreadsheets are riddled with errors: wrong values, incorrect VAT codes, wrong categories
  • Month-end is routinely slowed by poor quality submissions and missing receipts
  • Creates tension between employees and the business when payments are delayed

Corporate cards avoid staff laying out personal funds, but introduce their own problems — particularly around visibility and control.

  • Lack of real-time visibility — transactions only reconciled monthly, data weeks out of date
  • High credit limits make spending difficult to control; unauthorised spend goes undetected
  • Mid-month statement cut-off creates additional reconciliation complexity at month-end
  • No category-level controls — employees may incur spend that exceeds company policy

Chapter 3

Implement expense policies that cut out the extra work

Only with a clear policy can staff spend confidently on the company's behalf. Only with a clear policy can your finance team have a consistent template for accepting or rejecting claims — without detective work.

Good expense policy should be housed on the company intranet, included in HRMS software, and emailed to all staff. It needs to be clearly signposted — not buried in a 60-page employee handbook nobody reads.

What a complete expense policy covers

  • Spending limits per employee, department and category — e.g. £50/day meals, £150/night accommodation
  • Approval workflow: who authorises what, at what spend threshold, and within what timeframe
  • Eligible and ineligible expense categories, described in plain language with examples
  • Receipt requirements: originals required for all claims above £10, stored for a minimum of 6 years
  • Reimbursement timeline: payment within 7 days of an approved submission
  • Mileage rates aligned to current HMRC advisory rates
  • Policy for international travel: which currencies, exchange rate sources, and daily per diem limits

The questions to ask when evaluating any tool

  • Can you set spending limits by category, individual and department — plus time-based limits (daily, weekly, monthly)?
  • Can you create custom rules: forbidding weekend spend, restricting by geography, banning cash withdrawals?
  • Does it come with integrated payment cards, or can you connect existing ones?
  • Can you customise merchant categories beyond the pre-set list?

  • Is the submission process genuinely straightforward, or does it require training?
  • Does the mobile app go beyond receipt capture — can employees request funds, track spend, and submit reports from it?
  • Does the software automatically extract transaction details from receipt photos?
  • Does it notify employees when information is missing from a receipt or report?
  • Does it support multi-currency expense reports for international travel?

  • Can it export in multiple formats — PDF and CSV at minimum?
  • Does it integrate natively with Xero, QuickBooks, NetSuite or Sage?
  • Does it have an open API for custom integrations with your existing systems?
  • Can you filter management reports by time period, expense type, user and department?

  • Does it support a multi-stage approval workflow configurable by team or spend level?
  • Does it automatically flag missing VAT details before claims reach month-end?
  • Is mileage tracking built in, aligned to current HMRC advisory rates?
  • Are receipts stored securely and retrievably for the HMRC-required 6 years?
  • Is it MTD compliant and certified for connection to HMRC-approved accounting software?
