Standard Accordion

Spend management is the process of tracking, controlling, and optimising business expenditure across all company spending channels. It encompasses everything from employee expenses and supplier invoices to subscription services and corporate card transactions.

A robust spend management system gives finance teams real-time visibility into where money is going, enabling faster month-end closes and better budget forecasting.

Soldo connects natively with Xero, QuickBooks, NetSuite, and Sage. Transactions sync automatically, complete with receipts, VAT codes, and account mapping — eliminating manual reconciliation entirely.

Finance managers can set per-card spending limits, restrict merchant categories, lock cards instantly, and define budget rules per team or project. All controls are applied in real time from the web dashboard or mobile app.

What is Soldo?

Soldo is a spend management platform that combines prepaid Mastercard cards with a real-time control dashboard. Finance teams gain full visibility into company spending the moment it happens — not weeks later.

  • Prepaid Mastercards for every team and project
  • Real-time transaction data with receipt matching
  • Automated bookkeeping sync to Xero, QuickBooks, Sage
  • Custom rules, budgets, and merchant controls

Built for finance teams

Soldo gives CFOs and finance managers the controls they need without creating friction for the rest of the business. Budget rules, approval workflows, and automated reconciliation all live in one place.

  • Month-end close in hours, not days
  • Automated VAT coding and receipt matching
  • Real-time budget vs actuals across all teams

Empowering teams to spend wisely

With their own Soldo card, employees don't need to use personal funds or wait for reimbursements. Managers set the rules upfront, so teams can operate independently within defined guardrails.

Connect your existing tools

Soldo integrates with the accounting software you already use. Native connections to Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, and NetSuite mean transactions flow directly into your books — no manual exports, no data entry.

Why spend management matters more than ever

Business spending is no longer confined to a single company credit card or a carefully managed petty cash tin. Today, teams buy SaaS tools, book travel, purchase supplies, and entertain clients — often across dozens of different payment methods and currencies.

The result? Finance teams are perpetually catching up. Month-end reconciliation becomes a detective exercise, and the finance director's dashboard shows historical data, not what's happening right now.

The cost of poor spend visibility

When businesses lack real-time spend visibility, the consequences compound. A 2024 study by Barclaycard found that 62% of UK finance managers discovered unexpected expenditure at month-end that they had no prior knowledge of. Each incident costs an average of £1,200 in finance team time to investigate and resolve.
